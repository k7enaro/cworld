Overview


What are they?

A cactus is a member of the plant family Cactaceae, that is made up of 127 genera and nearly 1800 species of the order Caryophyllales.The word itself is derived through latin, from the Ancient Greek kaktos, a name that was first used by Theophrastus for a spiny plant whose identity is now not certin. they come in various shapes and sizes. Most can survive in very dry environments, even some has been discovered in the driest places on earth like the Atacama deserts. cacti are also very adaptable to conserve water which means they can also survive in wet conditions, in fact most cacti are succulents which means they have flesh and thickened parts adapted for water storage.

Why are they important?

Cactus is not just a plant, apart from it's many species with different shapes, looks and size, they are also very beneficial for Humans and Animals. Cacti are used as construction materials. Living cactus fences are employed as barricades around buildings to prevent people breaking in. They are also used to corral animals. Cacti have many other uses. They are used for human food and as fodder for animals, usually after burning off their spines. In addition to their use as psychoactive agents, some cacti are employed in herbal medicine. The practice of using various species of Opuntia in this way has spread from the Americas, where they naturally occur, to other regions where they grow, such as India.
